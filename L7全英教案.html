龍騰版 L7 TED Talks: Spreading Ideas for a Better World

全英語教案（EMI）· Claude 根據課文生成 · 含完整師生對話示範

年級 高一
節數 2 節課 · 各 50 分鐘
教學語言 全英語（EMI）
課本頁碼 P.153–P.159
口說形式 Pair Work + 個人發表
學習目標
第一節課（50 min）
第二節課（50 min）
課程總覽
OBJECTIVE 01 · 閱讀
Students can identify the main idea and supporting details of an informational text about TED Talks.
OBJECTIVE 02 · 詞彙
Students can use key vocabulary (seek, audience, concentrate, attention span, numerous, fascination) in context.
OBJECTIVE 03 · 文法
Students can use the keep / leave / find + O + OC sentence pattern to express states and observations.
OBJECTIVE 04 · 口說
Students can share a personal opinion about a topic they care about in English, using at least 3 sentences.
OBJECTIVE 05 · 思辨
Students can explain why TED limits talks to 18 minutes and connect this to their own experience of attention span.
OBJECTIVE 06 · 寫作
Students can write a 2–3 sentence Exit Ticket response in English summarizing what they learned.
本課核心問題 Essential Question
"If you could give an 18-minute TED Talk, what idea would you want to spread — and why?"
這個問題貫穿兩節課，第一節引入，第二節作為口說任務的核心。
0–8 min
Warm-Up: Have You Watched a TED Talk?
全班 · 師生對話
▶
目的：激活先備知識，建立學習動機，引導學生思考「演講」的概念。
完整師生對話示範
T
"Good morning, everyone. Before we start, I have a question for you. Have you ever watched a TED Talk?"
環顧全班，停頓等待回應
S
"Yes." / "No." / "I've seen some on YouTube."
T
"Great! For those who have — what was it about? Can you share in one sentence?"
指名 2–3 位學生回答
S
"It was about how to be happy." / "Someone talked about climate change."
T
"Interesting! TED Talks cover almost every topic — from science to art to everyday life. Today, we're going to read about TED and find out what makes it so special. Let's start with one key question:"
T
"Why do you think TED Talks are only 18 minutes long?"
寫在黑板上，讓學生先猜，不給答案——第一節結束前回來確認
S
"Because people get bored?" / "To save time?" / "I don't know."
T
"Good guesses! Keep this question in mind — the answer is in today's reading. Let's find out together."
8–15 min
Pre-Reading: TED Prediction Activity
Pair Work · 預測策略
▶
目的：使用預測策略降低閱讀焦慮，同時練習口說。
老師指令示範
T
"Now, look at the infographic on page 153. Don't read the text yet — just look at the pictures and numbers."
T
"Turn to your partner. You have 2 minutes. Tell your partner: What do you think this article is about? Use this sentence starter:"
板書 / 投影
"I think this article is about _____ because I can see _____."
T
"Ready? Go!"
計時 2 分鐘，巡視教室聆聽，記下有趣的想法
T
"Time's up. Who wants to share? What did you and your partner predict?"
S
"We think it's about public speaking because we can see a person on a stage."
T
"Great prediction! Let's see if you're right. Open your books to page 155."
15–35 min
Reading: Topic Sentence Strategy
個人 + Pair Check · 閱讀策略
▶
目的：練習找主題句，建立段落理解能力。
老師指令示範
T
"We're going to read pages 155 to 159. As you read, I want you to do one thing: underline the topic sentence of each paragraph."
T
"Remember — a topic sentence tells you the main idea of the paragraph. It's usually the first sentence, but not always. Ready? You have 10 minutes. Go."
計時，教師巡視，觀察學生在哪裡停下來
Pair Check（閱讀後）
T
"OK, time's up. Compare with your partner — did you underline the same sentences? If you disagree, discuss why."
給 2 分鐘討論
T
"Let's check together. Paragraph 1 — who can read the topic sentence they underlined?"
S
"TED stands for Technology, Entertainment and Design."
T
"Good. That's the topic sentence — it tells us what TED stands for, which is the main idea of that paragraph. Now paragraph 2..."
繼續逐段確認，重點放在學生選錯的段落
如果學生選了細節句而非主題句，引導他們問：「Does this sentence give a main idea, or is it a detail that supports another idea?」
35–45 min
Comprehension Check: 18 Minutes — Why?
全班討論 · 回應開場問題
▶
目的：回應課堂開場的核心問題，確認閱讀理解。
完整師生對話示範
T
"OK — remember the question I wrote on the board at the beginning? Why are TED Talks 18 minutes long? Now that you've read the article, who can answer?"
S
"Because people can't concentrate for a long time."
T
"Exactly! What's the word for how long we can stay focused?"
S
"Attention span?"
T
"Perfect. Attention span. So TED uses 18 minutes because it fits within the human attention span. Does that make sense to you? Do you agree?"
S
"Yes, I think so." / "Sometimes I can focus longer if the topic is interesting."
T
"Great point! The topic matters. TED picks topics that are worth spreading — that's their motto. What's TED's motto?"
S
"Ideas worth spreading."
T
"Exactly. Keep that in mind for next class — you'll use it."
45–50 min
Exit Ticket: One Thing You Learned
個人書寫 · 2–3 句英文
▶
目的：確認本節學習成效，練習英文書寫。
投影 / 板書
Write 2–3 sentences in English:
1. One thing I learned about TED today is _____.
2. I think the 18-minute rule is a good / bad idea because _____.
T
"Before you leave, write 2–3 sentences on a piece of paper. You have 5 minutes. This is your exit ticket — I'll read them after class to see what you understood."
收集後快速瀏覽，找出常見的誤解或有趣的觀點，下節課開頭用來引入文法活動。
0–8 min
Warm-Up: Vocabulary Review Game
全班 · 快問快答
▶
目的：複習上節課詞彙，活化課堂氣氛。
seek
/siːk/ v.
to look for or try to get something
"She seeks to improve her English every day."
audience
/ˈɔːdiəns/ n.
a group of people who watch or listen
"The audience applauded after the talk."
concentrate
/ˈkɒnsntreɪt/ v.
to focus your attention on something
"It's hard to concentrate in a noisy room."
attention span
n. phrase
the length of time you can stay focused
"Children have a shorter attention span than adults."
numerous
/ˈnjuːmərəs/ adj.
many; a large number of
"There are numerous TED Talks online."
fascination
/ˌfæsɪˈneɪʃn/ n.
a strong interest or attraction
"His fascination with science led him to TED."
快問快答遊戲示範
T
"Let's do a quick vocabulary warm-up. I'll give you a definition — you tell me the word. Ready?"
T
"The length of time you can stay focused on something. What's the word?"
S
"Attention span!"
T
"Good! Next — a strong interest or attraction toward something."
S
"Fascination!"
T
"Perfect. Last one — many, a large number of."
S
"Numerous!"
T
"Excellent! You remember everything from last class. Let's move on to grammar."
8–25 min
Grammar: keep / leave / find + O + OC
教師講解 + 練習
▶
目的：理解並練習 keep/leave/find + 受詞 + 受詞補語的句型。
句型介紹示範
T
"Today we're looking at a very useful sentence pattern: keep, leave, and find — followed by an object and an object complement. Let me show you what I mean."
板書 / 投影
keep + O + OC → "TED keeps its talks short."
leave + O + OC → "A good talk leaves the audience thinking."
find + O + OC → "Many people find TED Talks inspiring."
T
"Notice the pattern — after keep, leave, or find, we have a noun (the object) and then an adjective or a verb-ing (the object complement). The complement describes the state of the object."
T
"Let's try one together. How would you say: 'Watching TED Talks makes me feel inspired'? Use the find pattern."
S
"I find TED Talks... inspiring?"
T
"Exactly! 'I find TED Talks inspiring.' The object is 'TED Talks,' and the complement is 'inspiring.' Perfect."
配對練習（Pair Work · 5 min）
練習題
Complete the sentences with your partner:
1. The 18-minute rule keeps the audience _____ (adj./V-ing).
2. A boring talk can leave the listeners _____ (adj./V-ing).
3. I find learning English _____.
4. TED keeps its motto _____ — "Ideas Worth Spreading."
T
"Work with your partner. Complete these four sentences using the keep/leave/find pattern. You have 4 minutes."
T
"Let's check. Number 1 — who wants to share?"
S
"The 18-minute rule keeps the audience focused."
T
"Great! 'Focused' works perfectly. Any other answers? 'Engaged'? 'Interested'? All good options."
25–42 min
Speaking Task: My 1-Minute TED Talk
Pair Work → 個人發表
▶
目的：整合本課詞彙和句型，練習口說表達個人觀點。
任務說明示範
T
"Now it's your turn to be a TED speaker. Here's your challenge:"
投影 / 板書
My 1-Minute TED Talk

Topic: "If I could give a TED Talk, I would talk about _____ because _____."

You must use:
· At least 2 vocabulary words from this lesson
· At least 1 sentence with keep / leave / find
· At least 3 sentences total
T
"Step 1: Think about your topic for 1 minute. It can be anything you care about — music, sports, food, the environment, anything."
給學生 1 分鐘思考
T
"Step 2: Share with your partner. Each person speaks for about 1 minute. Your partner listens and then asks one question. Ready? Go!"
計時 4 分鐘（各 2 分鐘），巡視教室給予鼓勵
個人發表（全班）
T
"Great job, everyone. Now I'd like to invite a few people to share with the class. Who's brave enough to go first?"
等待自願者，若無人舉手，可說："I heard a great idea from [seat number]. Would you like to share?"
S
"If I could give a TED Talk, I would talk about mental health because I find this topic important. I think many students keep their feelings hidden, and that can leave them feeling lonely. We need to talk about it more."
T
"That was excellent! I noticed you used 'find' and 'keep' and 'leave' — all three patterns! And your topic is definitely worth spreading. Thank you."
具體指出學生做對的地方，不只說 good job
若時間允許，請 3–4 位學生發表。發表後可讓全班投票最喜歡哪個 TED Talk 主題。
42–50 min
Exit Ticket + Lesson Wrap-Up
個人書寫 + 全班總結
▶
目的：總結兩節課學習，確認掌握程度。
課程總結示範
T
"We're almost done. Let's look back at what we learned over these two classes."
T
"What does TED stand for?"
S
"Technology, Entertainment and Design."
T
"And what's TED's motto?"
S
"Ideas worth spreading."
T
"Perfect. And today you all shared your own ideas worth spreading. That's what TED is all about."
Exit Ticket（最後 5 分鐘）
Write in English:
1. The grammar pattern I learned today is _____. Example: _____.
2. If I were a TED speaker, I would talk about _____ because _____.
T
"Last thing — write your exit ticket. Two sentences. You have 5 minutes. This is your chance to show me what you learned. Go ahead."
T
"Great work today, everyone. See you next time!"
