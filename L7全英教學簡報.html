Slide 4 · Reading Strategy
READING STRATEGY

Find the Topic Sentence

What is a Topic Sentence?
A topic sentence states the main idea of a paragraph.

It is usually the first sentence, but not always.

Ask yourself: "What is this paragraph mainly about?"
Your Task
Read pages 155–159.
Underline the topic sentence of each paragraph.
⏱ You have 10 minutes.
Then compare with your partner.
Example
"TED stands for Technology, Entertainment and Design."
✓ This is the topic sentence — it tells us what TED stands for.

Slide 6 · Grammar
GRAMMAR

keep / leave / find + O + OC

keep + O + OC
TED keeps its talks short.
The rule keeps the audience focused.
leave + O + OC
A boring talk can leave the audience sleeping.
A great story leaves people inspired.
find + O + OC
Many people find TED Talks inspiring.
I find this topic fascinating.

DISCUSSION

My 1-Minute TED Talk

"If you could give an 18-minute TED Talk, what idea would you want to spread — and why?"
Use at least 2 vocabulary words from today · Use one keep / leave / find sentence
STEP 1 · 1 min
Think about your topic. It can be anything you care about.
STEP 2 · 4 min
Share with your partner. Each person speaks for ~1 minute. Partner asks 1 question.
STEP 3 · Class
3–4 students share with the class. Class votes for the most interesting idea.
Sentence starter: "If I could give a TED Talk, I would talk about _____ because _____."
