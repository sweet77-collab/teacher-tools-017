Part I · Multiple Choice
Q1 · 主旨理解 · 中
According to the passage, TED was established mainly to
（A）provide entertainment for technology workers
（B）create a platform for sharing ideas across different fields
（C）organize technology conferences for engineers only
（D）promote science fiction books and films
✓ 答案：B　TED 的宗旨是 "Ideas Worth Spreading"，目的是跨領域分享想法。
🎯 干擾：A 混淆 Technology 讓人以為只針對科技工作者；C 縮小範圍為工程師；D 與 Cameron 演講混淆。
Q2 · 細節理解 · 易
How long is each TED Talk limited to?
（A）Fifteen minutes
（B）Twenty minutes
（C）Eighteen minutes
（D）Thirty minutes
✓ 答案：C　課文明確說明每場 TED Talk 限制在 18 分鐘以內。
🎯 干擾：A（15分）和B（20分）是最接近的數字；D（30分）是常見演講長度。
Q3 · 推論 · 中
Why does TED limit each talk to eighteen minutes?
（A）To allow more speakers to present at each conference
（B）Because the audience's attention span is limited
（C）To make the talks suitable for TV broadcast
（D）Because speakers find shorter talks easier to memorize
✓ 答案：B　課文說明 18 分鐘是因為人類注意力有限，讓講者專注核心訊息。
🎯 干擾：A 是合理猜測但課文未提；C 加入 TV 元素；D 從講者角度切入似乎合理但非重點。
Q4 · 詞彙 · 易
The word "increasingly" in "TED Talks have an increasingly large collection of videos" is closest in meaning to
（A）suddenly
（B）more and more
（C）surprisingly
（D）recently
✓ 答案：B　"Increasingly" 表示「越來越」，強調持續增長趨勢。
🎯 干擾：A 和 C 改變語氣；D 只說時間點沒有「持續增加」的意涵。
Q5 · 語意推論 · 難
What does "standing on the shoulders of a giant" mean in the passage?
（A）TED speakers are physically taller than average people
（B）Viewers gain a broader perspective from others' knowledge
（C）TED Talks are only for tall and intelligent people
（D）The audience must stand during TED conferences
✓ 答案：B　「站在巨人的肩膀上」是比喻，藉由他人知識獲得更寬廣視野。
🎯 干擾：A 和 D 是字面解讀；C 加入歧視性條件讓學生思考是否合理。
Q6 · 細節 · 中
Which person is NOT mentioned as a TED Talk speaker in the passage?
（A）Jamie Oliver
（B）James Cameron
（C）Elon Musk
（D）Bill Gates
✓ 答案：C　課文提到 Jamie Oliver、James Cameron 和 Bill Gates，沒有 Elon Musk。
🎯 干擾：Elon Musk 是知名科技人物，學生容易誤以為他也在課文中。
Q7 · 細節 · 易
What was the topic of Jamie Oliver's TED Talk?
（A）Climate change and the environment
（B）Teaching children about healthy food
（C）New cooking techniques for restaurants
（D）The future of the food industry
✓ 答案：B　課文說 Jamie Oliver 的演講關於教孩子認識健康食物。
🎯 干擾：C 和 D 都與食物有關但偏商業面；A 是當代熱門話題容易聯想。
Q8 · 全文理解 · 難
What is the main idea of this passage?
（A）Famous people give better speeches than ordinary people
（B）TED Talks are an effective way to spread valuable ideas
（C）Technology conferences are more popular than ever
（D）Eighteen minutes is the perfect length for any presentation
✓ 答案：B　本文主旨是介紹 TED Talks 如何有效傳播有價值的想法。
🎯 干擾：A 以偏概全；C 只聚焦科技；D 把18分鐘過度延伸到所有簡報。
Q9 · 詞彙應用 · 中
The word "seek" in "people seek to broaden their knowledge" means
（A）avoid
（B）pretend
（C）try to find or obtain
（D）refuse
✓ 答案：C　"Seek" 意為「尋求、努力獲得」。
🎯 干擾：A 和 D 是反義；B 是語意陷阱。
Q10 · 推論 · 難
Based on the passage, which statement about TED Talks is most accurate?
（A）They are only for people who work in technology
（B）They focus on entertainment rather than education
（C）They bring together diverse ideas to inspire a global audience
（D）They require speakers to have a university degree
✓ 答案：C　TED 跨越科技、娛樂、設計，面向全球傳播多元想法。
🎯 干擾：A 限縮到科技領域；B 混淆 TED 的 E（Entertainment）為娛樂節目；D 是無中生有的條件。
